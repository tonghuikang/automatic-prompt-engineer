85 How do semantic networks work? Semantic networks are a way of representing knowledge in a structured, graphical format. In a semantic network, each node represents a concept or object, and the links between nodes represent the relationships between those concepts. These relationships can be of different types, such as "is-a" (for hierarchical relationships), "part-of" (for part-whole relationships), or more specific semantic relations like "causes", "uses", or "located-in".

The structure of a semantic network allows for efficient storage and retrieval of information, as well as inference and reasoning. By traversing the network, you can follow the connections between concepts to understand their relationships and draw conclusions. Semantic networks are used in various applications, such as natural language processing, knowledge representation, and expert systems.
